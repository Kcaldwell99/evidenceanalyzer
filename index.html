What Evidentix™ does

Built for digital-evidence disputes where authenticity, source, timing, or manipulation may affect liability, credibility, or outcome.

🛡️

Integrity Verification

Generates a SHA-256 fingerprint at upload. Any subsequent alteration produces a different hash — creating an objective, documentable tamper-detection baseline from the moment evidence is received.

🔍

Comparison Analysis

Runs perceptual hash comparison between two files — original and suspect. Flags divergence points, similarity percentage, and visual differences in a structured side-by-side report.

Chain-of-Custody Reporting

Every access, upload, and analysis action is timestamped and logged. The output is a formatted PDF report — suitable for use as an exhibit, discovery response, or expert disclosure foundation.

Common questions

Can Evidentix™ tell whether an image or video was altered?

The platform reviews available metadata, file characteristics, and hash-level comparison signals, then reports its findings and the limits of what can be concluded from the submitted file. It does not replace a retained forensic examiner for high-stakes testimony, but it provides a defensible, documented baseline at a fraction of the cost.

What file formats are supported?

Images (JPEG, PNG, TIFF, HEIC, WebP), video files (MP4, MOV, AVI, MKV), and PDFs. For best results, upload the original native file rather than a screenshot, texted copy, or social-media download — those versions often lose or alter important metadata.

Is the report usable in court?

Reports are formatted for use as an exhibit, discovery response, or foundation for expert disclosure. How the report is used — and its admissibility — depends on the jurisdiction, the rules of evidence, and the specific matter. The platform does not provide legal advice.
